# **Avogadro Molecular Editor**

**Avogadro** is a free cross-platform molecular editor and visualizer for computational chemistry, molecular modeling, and materials science with advanced 3D structure manipulation capabilities.

Avogadro typically supports:
- Interactive 3D molecule building, editing, and optimization with multi-threaded rendering.
- Support for 80+ chemical file formats via Open Babel integration for import/export.
- Crystal structure editing, periodic boundary conditions, and surface/mesh generation.
- Plugin architecture with Python/C++ extensions for custom force fields and analysis tools.

## License and Cost

Avogadro is open-source under GPL v2 license, completely free across Windows, Linux, and macOS platforms.

## Limitations

Primarily focused on molecular visualization and editing rather than advanced quantum chemistry calculations or large-scale molecular dynamics simulations.

# **RDKit Cheminformatics Toolkit**

**RDKit** is an open-source cheminformatics toolkit for computational chemistry, drug discovery, and chemical informatics with comprehensive molecule manipulation capabilities.

RDKit typically supports:
- 2D/3D molecule generation, conformer search, and molecular descriptor calculation.
- Substructure searching, fingerprint generation, and similarity analysis.
- Reaction processing, retrosynthesis, and combinatorial chemistry enumeration.
- Machine learning integration with scikit-learn, TensorFlow, and PyTorch bindings.

## License and Cost

RDKit is open-source under BSD license, free Python/C++/Java bindings for commercial/research use.

## Limitations

No built-in 3D visualization (requires integration with tools like PyMOL or NGLView).

## Useful References

- **[RDKit Official Documentation](https://www.rdkit.org)**  
- **[RDKit GitHub](https://github.com/rdkit/rdkit)**  
- **[Cookbook](https://www.rdkit.org/docs/Cookbook.html)**  
- **[Tutorials](https://github.com/rdkit/rdkit-tutorials)**  
- **[Getting Started](https://www.rdkit.org/docs/GettingStartedInPython.html)**

# **DWSIM Chemical Process Simulator**

**DWSIM** is an open-source CAPE-OPEN compliant chemical process simulator for steady-state modeling of petrochemical, oil & gas, and chemical processes.

DWSIM typically supports:
- Thermodynamic models including Peng-Robinson, NRTL, UNIQUAC, and PC-SAFT.
- Unit operations for distillation, reactors, heat exchangers, and separators.
- Flowsheet sensitivity analysis, optimization, and batch processing capabilities.
- Python scripting and Excel integration for custom calculations.

## License and Cost

DWSIM is open-source under LGPL license, free across Windows, Linux, and macOS with professional support available.

## Limitations

Steady-state simulation only (no dynamic simulation). Limited to supported thermodynamic models.

## Useful References

- **[DWSIM Official Website](https://dwsim.org)**  
- **[DWSIM GitHub](https://github.com/DWSIM-Project/DWSIM)**  
- **[Documentation](https://dwsim.org/wiki/index.php?title=Main_Page)**  
- **[Tutorials](https://dwsim.org/wiki/index.php?title=Tutorials)**  
- **[CAPE-OPEN Compliance](https://dwsim.org/cape-open)**

# **CP2K Quantum Chemistry Package**

**CP2K** is an open-source quantum chemistry and solid-state physics software using Gaussian and plane wave Density Functional Theory (DFT) methods.

CP2K typically supports:
- DFT with hybrid functionals, RPA, MP2, and GW approximations.
- Ab initio molecular dynamics, vibrational analysis, and Ehrenfest dynamics.
- Linear scaling DFT for large systems and periodic boundary conditions.
- GPU acceleration and massively parallel execution capabilities.

## License and Cost

CP2K is open-source under GPL v2 license, completely free for academic and research use.

## Limitations

Steep learning curve requiring quantum chemistry background. Limited basis set support compared to commercial codes.
